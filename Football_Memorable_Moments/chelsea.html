Chelsea - 2012 Champions League

2012 marked a special period in the history of Chelsea football club. It was the first time that the club won the Champions League having to beat Bayern Munich at the Allianz arena. Needless to say it was no cake-walk for the club.

Outside of the Champions League Chelsea had troubles in the English Premier League. Having gone through a rough run of form with 3 wins out of 12 matches the club fired André Villas-Boas leaving the interim manager Roberto Di Matteo in charge. As well as this Chelsea had just signed Fernando Torress for £50 million and he himself was experiencing a rough run of form. However, the striker proved his metal by scoring an important goal at the Nou Camp against Barcelona in the Champions League semi-final making the match 2-2 allowing Chelsea to progress 3-2 on aggregate.

Torress celebrating decisive goal against Barcelona

After the hard-fought semi-finals having to overcome the best team in the world Chelsea now had to pull off another massive upset by beating Bayern Munich at the Allianz arena. What an upset it was. With the game going to penalties following a header from Drogba sending the game into extra-time it was the Ivorian striker who stepped up to slot away the decisive penalty, realising the long-standing ambition of the Club. This victory made Chelsea the first London club to win the Champions League and with Tottenham's loss to Liverpool in the 2019 final Chelsea are still the only London club to have won the Champions league.
